# Brand — Aakhara

## Name
**Aakhara** (Sanskrit/Hindi: आखरा — "the arena, the learning ground"). Domain: `aakhara.com` (pending). Sales/edtech enterprise product; pilot with CodingNinjas (CN).

The root *aakhara* — a wrestler's training ground — carries the exact metaphor we want: a place for disciplined, repeated practice, where one earns skill through showing up. Voice training fits: you practice, you're coached, you get better.

## One-liner
Voice-practice training for sales teams — disciplined, coached, measurable. Built for the first 100 calls of every new rep.

## Story
New sales reps are thrown into live calls before they're ready. Onboarding is PDFs, mock role-plays that don't scale, and a manager's time. Quality suffers, deals are lost, reps quit early. Aakhara is the training ground — a voice-AI simulation rep, calibrated scenario library, coaching feedback loop, and management dashboard to see who's ready for live calls.

First pilot: CodingNinjas (CN) — their sales reps practice objection-handling, discovery calls, and renewal conversations in Aakhara before getting routed to real leads.

The Sanskrit name is deliberate — positions Aakhara in the same cultural register as Indian edtech buyers expect (BYJU's, Unacademy, CodingNinjas), while the aesthetic stays modern-technical enough to read as a *tool*, not a *course*.

## Mission
- Shrink the time-to-first-good-call for new sales reps from 30 days to 5.
- Standardize objection-handling quality across distributed sales teams.
- Make practice measurable — managers see rep readiness scores before approving live-call access.

## Visual voice
**Disciplined + warm + measurable.** Axis points:
- **Disciplined** — structured practice flows, clear metrics, readiness scores. Not gamified — professional.
- **Warm** — saffron/amber accent acknowledges the Indian cultural register + the encouragement-not-punishment framing.
- **Measurable** — dashboards, score arcs, time-to-proficiency charts. Numbers carry the story.
- **Modern-technical** — no "traditional Indian ornament" overdose; contemporary sans-serif + Devanagari at tasteful proportions.

## Audience
- **Primary**: sales training managers / L&D leaders at Indian edtech + SaaS companies. 30–50 year olds, MBA-trained, outcome-focused.
- **Secondary**: new sales reps (22–28, often first jobs), who need practice that feels serious but forgiving.
- **Tertiary**: startup founders setting up their first sales process.

NOT: consumer. NOT: individual-rep self-improvement (that's a different product — coaching not training).

## Competitors / differentiation
- vs. Chorus / Gong (conversation intelligence) — those analyze real calls; Aakhara trains BEFORE real calls.
- vs. Second Nature AI — similar voice-practice category; Aakhara differentiates on **India-first aesthetic + CN-calibrated scenarios + Hindi bilingual scenarios**.
- vs. BYJU's sales training (internal) — they built in-house; Aakhara is the product they'd have bought.

Differentiator: **India-first + bilingual-capable + deeply integrated with Vagary Voice infrastructure**. The voice layer is battle-tested (from Vagary Voice); the differentiator is the training content + scoring model.
